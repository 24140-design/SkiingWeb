What You Need Before You Go


Clothing



What to Wear & Where to Buy

Base Layer
Worn against your skin, pulls sweat away so you stay dry. Go for merino wool or synthetic, never cotton as it holds moisture and will freeze you out.
Amazon REI


Mid Layer (Fleece)
Traps warm air close to your body. A zip-up fleece is ideal so you can vent when you're working hard on the slopes.
Amazon REI


Outer Shell (Jacket & Pants)
Your protection against wind, snow, and wet. You will fall and you will land in snow, so waterproof is non-negotiable.
Amazon REI


Gloves or Mittens
Cold hands turn painful fast and will ruin your day. Waterproof and insulated is the minimum. Mittens are warmer, gloves give better pole grip.
Amazon REI


Goggles
Protect your eyes from UV glare, wind, and flying snow. A bad pair will fog constantly so worth spending a little more here.
Amazon REI


Helmet
Non-negotiable. Beginners fall at unexpected angles and even slow-speed falls can hurt. Make sure it fits snug with no wobble.
Amazon REI


Ski Socks
Tall, thin merino wool socks designed for ski boots. Never double up as it restricts circulation and makes your feet colder.
Amazon REI


Neck Gaiter
Fills the gap between your helmet and jacket collar. Small item but makes a big difference on cold or windy days.
Amazon REI



Equipment



What You Need & Where to Rent

Skis
Shorter, wider skis are easier to control as a beginner. Resort staff will fit you to the right length when you rent, so don't stress about this one.
Skiset Evo


Ski Boots
Snug but not painful. A good fit gives you control and a bad fit means sore feet and sloppy turns. Always get fitted in person at the resort.
Skiset Evo


Poles
Help with balance and timing your turns. When holding them your elbow should sit at roughly 90 degrees. Usually included in rental packages.
Skiset Evo


Snowboard (alternative to skis)
Prefer boarding? Go for a wider, softer board as a beginner, it's more forgiving on falls. Available at most resort rental shops.
Skiset Evo


💰 Rent, Don't Buy

A full ski setup costs $800 to $2000+ to buy. As a beginner, renting is the smart move. You get properly fitted gear and won't be stuck with equipment you outgrow after one season.





Top 3 Mountains for Beginners



Explore resorts globally renowned for the best gentle slopes, top-tier ski schools, and beginner-friendly facilities.

Les Gets, France




Les Gets is widely considered one of the best ski resorts for beginners in the world. It offers a fantastic, designated beginner area called the "Mappys Area," which is completely free to access and safely tucked away from the busier slopes. The village is charming, traditional, and has a very welcoming atmosphere. With excellent ski schools and plenty of wide, gentle tree-lined runs, it’s the perfect place to build your confidence on the snow.

Soldeu, Andorra




Soldeu is a fantastic choice for those just starting out. Located in the massive Grandvalira ski area, it features an award-winning English-speaking ski school, making it incredibly popular with international beginners. The nursery slopes are exceptionally wide and forgiving, allowing you to learn without feeling crowded. Plus, Andorra is known for being budget-friendly and having a fun, relaxed après-ski scene for after a long day of learning.

Obergurgl, Austria




Known as the "Diamond of the Alps," Obergurgl is one of Europe's highest ski villages, which means snow is practically guaranteed. Beginners love it here because the nursery slopes are located right next to the village center, meaning no long treks with heavy gear. The runs are wide, uncrowded, and perfectly groomed. It’s a slightly quieter, more premium resort that lets you learn in peace accompanied by stunning Austrian views.
