Val d'Anniviers, Switzerland




Val d’Anniviers is a stunning valley in the Swiss Alps, home to picturesque villages like Grimentz, Zinal, and St-Luc. It features a wide range of ski slopes, from gentle runs perfect for beginners to challenging pistes for advanced skiers, all benefiting from reliable snow due to the high altitude. The ski area is peaceful and less crowded than many big resorts, offering a traditional alpine atmosphere with charming wooden chalets and breathtaking views of famous peaks like the Weisshorn. It’s an ideal destination for anyone looking to enjoy authentic Swiss skiing surrounded by beautiful mountain scenery.

Bansko, Bulgaria




Bansko is one of the premier ski destinations in Eastern Europe, set at the foot of the majestic Pirin Mountains. The resort combines a charming old town filled with traditional stone houses and cozy taverns with a modern ski area offering a wide variety of slopes for all abilities. From gentle beginner runs to long, challenging red and black pistes descending from Todorka Peak, skiers can enjoy reliable snow conditions throughout the winter season. The well-developed lift system and lively après-ski scene make it both convenient and vibrant, while the surrounding alpine scenery provides a dramatic backdrop. Bansko is an excellent choice for those seeking great-value skiing with a blend of culture, comfort, and impressive mountain terrain.

La Grave, France




La Grave is a legendary alpine village nestled beneath the towering peaks of the French Alps, offering one of the most authentic and adventurous ski experiences in Europe. Overlooked by the dramatic glaciers of La Meije, the area is renowned for its vast, lift-accessed off-piste terrain rather than groomed pistes, attracting confident skiers and freeriders from around the world. Descents sweep through high-alpine glaciers, open powder fields, and steep couloirs, all set against breathtaking mountain scenery. With its traditional stone houses, quiet atmosphere, and absence of large-scale resort development, La Grave retains a raw, unspoiled charm.
